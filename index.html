The Tubes

What is this?

Hi, I'm Owen and I realize it's kind of an odd thing to request but if you happen to have an empty toothpaste tube laying around your bathroom—I need you to give it to me instead of throwing it in your garbage.

You see, for no particular reason, I was recently conducting a few experiments with Plaster of Paris to see what kinds of things you could do with it. At the time, I also had an empty toothpaste tube sitting in my bathroom so I poured plaster into it and the whole thing got hard as a rock, which I thought was kind of neat. I mean it just looks like a regular tube of toothpaste sitting there, but then you go to pick it up and you realize it doesn't feel the way you would expect it to.

So after that I started to wonder…what if you got a whole bunch of empty tubes together in a room and filled them all with plaster? How many could you collect? How would go about filling them all and then what would you do with a bunch of rock hard toothpaste tubes? How would you display them? A big pile? On a wall? What would it smell like?

And so these are the questions I now seek to answer and the only way I can do that is if you give me your empty toothpaste tube. We both know you're just going to toss it in the garbage once it gets down to that last little bit so what's the big deal? Just fill out the form down below and I'll come pick it up. You can even just leave it out at the end of your driveway if you want. Just remember to write something on it with a Sharpie because depending how they eventually get displayed you might be able to identify your tube later.

And if you have more than one tube then that's even better. Get your friends tubes and your neighbors tubes too.

Questions

What kind of tubes do you accept?

Any brand, any size. Travel tubes, family-sized tubes, whatever. Just make sure it's actually empty.

Does it need the cap?

Caps are good. If it still has the cap, leave it on.

Can I write something on my tube?

Yes. Grab a Sharpie and write your name, a date, a message, a drawing...whatever you want. It's your tube.

Will the tubes be displayed?

Yes. Once we've collected and filled all the tubes with plaster, the final masterpiece will be displayed somewhere in Charleston, West Virginia.

Has this been done before?

No. It's a first.

Is this some sort of political movement?

No.
